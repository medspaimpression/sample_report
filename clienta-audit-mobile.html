05 — The Exploit
Three Specific Opportunities

Each is grounded in a specific gap in competitor review data and a specific asset Client A already possesses.

1
Exploit 01 · Positioning
Own "Clinical Artistry" as a Category
Every competitor competes on warmth and ambiance. ElaMar leads on experience theater. NakedMD leads on price. Nobody owns clinical precision and artistic outcomes — and Client A's review language is already there: "freaking ARTIST," "most beautiful lip filler I've ever had," "results are unmatched." Push patients to talk about what they see in the mirror, not how the champagne tasted.
Action
Revise your review request to ask: "If you loved your results, would you share what you noticed in the first 2 weeks?"
2
Exploit 02 · Service Category
Activate Laser Body Treatments as a Standalone Category
Client A is the only practice in this competitive set with laser body treatment mentioned in reviews. Not one competitor surfaces this service. There is a patient segment who wants laser body treatments, believes med spas don't offer them, and has nowhere obvious to go. A targeted 4-week Instagram series would face zero local competitive noise.
Action
4 posts over 4 weeks featuring real patient outcomes. Boost each post $50–100. Target Nashville women 28–45 within 10 miles. Goal: 3+ consult requests in 30 days.
3
Exploit 03 · Review Engine
Build a Post-Appointment Review Ritual
A text from [Lead Injector] personally — 48 hours post-appointment, checking in on results and including a direct Google review link — would likely generate 3–4x your current monthly review volume. Patients who describe their injector as an "artist" will leave a review for that person. You're not asking for a favor — you're giving them a platform to brag about their results.
Action
Set up a 48-hour SMS automation via Vagaro or Podium attributed to [Lead Injector] personally. Script: "Hey [name], checking in on your results — I hope you're loving them. Would you mind sharing on Google? [link]"
