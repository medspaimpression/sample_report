05
AI Visibility

Google AI isn't reading your star rating. It's reading what patients say.

AI Overviews and tools like ChatGPT now synthesize review content to recommend local practices. The practices with the most specific, outcome-focused reviews are being surfaced. Practices with thin review profiles are being trained out.

Client A: 72 reviews · Not surfaced in AI results
Why this matters now

26% of patients now choose providers based on AI tools — nearly matching physician referrals at 28%. This is not a future problem. It is a current one. ElaMar is being trained into recommendations. Client A is not.

What AI is actually reading

AI Overviews don't aggregate star ratings — they synthesize specific patient language. "Best injector in Nashville." "Most natural lip filler I've had." Outcome phrases. Client A has these phrases. They exist in 72 reviews that almost no AI tool is finding.

The compounding problem

Every month ElaMar adds 15–18 reviews — many with outcome-specific language. Every month that language trains AI recommendations more deeply. The gap is not static. It is widening.

The fix

Volume alone isn't enough. Reviews need to contain outcome-specific language — what the patient noticed, what changed, what they'd tell a friend. A review request that prompts for outcomes changes what AI learns about your practice.

06
The Exploit

Three specific opportunities

Each is grounded in a specific gap in competitor review data and a specific asset Client A already possesses.

1
Exploit 01 · Positioning

Own "Clinical Artistry" as a Category

Every competitor competes on warmth and ambiance. ElaMar leads on experience theater. NakedMD leads on price. Nobody owns clinical precision and artistic outcomes — and Client A's review language is already there: "freaking ARTIST," "most beautiful lip filler I've ever had," "results are unmatched." Push patients to talk about what they see in the mirror, not how the champagne tasted.

Action

Revise your review request to ask: "If you loved your results, would you share what you noticed in the first 2 weeks?"

2
Exploit 02 · Service Category

Activate Laser Body Treatments as a Standalone Category

Client A is the only practice in this competitive set with laser body treatment mentioned in reviews. Not one competitor surfaces this service. There is a patient segment who wants laser body treatments, believes med spas don't offer them, and has nowhere obvious to go. A targeted 4-week Instagram series would face zero local competitive noise.

Action

4 posts over 4 weeks featuring real patient outcomes. Boost each post $50–100. Target Nashville women 28–45 within 10 miles. Goal: 3+ consult requests in 30 days.

3
Exploit 03 · Review Engine

Build a Post-Appointment Review Ritual

A text from Lead Injector personally — 48 hours post-appointment, checking in on results and including a direct Google review link — would likely generate 3–4x your current monthly review volume. Patients who describe their injector as an "artist" will leave a review for that person. You're not asking for a favor — you're giving them a platform to brag about their results. This also directly improves AI visibility by generating outcome-specific language at volume.

Action

Set up a 48-hour SMS automation via Vagaro or Podium attributed to Lead Injector personally. Script: "Hey [name], checking in on your results — I hope you're loving them. Would you mind sharing on Google? [link]"
